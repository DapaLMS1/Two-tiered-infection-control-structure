A Two-Tiered Approach to Comprehensive Risk Control

Tier 1 Foundational or Universal Risk Controls

Tier 1: Foundational or Universal Risk Controls

These are the minimum, baseline safety practices used for all routine operations and individuals, regardless of specific known risks. They're the everyday precautions dental practices put in place:

  • Infection Control: Standard Precautions like hand hygiene, PPE (gloves, masks), and instrument sterilisation for all patients.
  • Other Risks:
    • Ergonomics: Ergonomic setups and staff stretching.
    • Chemical Safety: Proper storage/labelling and ventilation.
    • Physical Hazards: Clear walkways, clean spills, routine checks.

Tier 2 Enhanced or Specific Risk Controls

Tier 2: Enhanced or Specific Risk Controls

These are additional, targeted measures used when elevated hazards are identified. They build upon Tier 1 controls for added protection:

  • Infection Control: Transmission-Based Precautions (e.g., P2 respirators, isolation protocols for infectious patients).
  • Other Risks:
    • Ergonomics: Special tools or layout adjustments for staff in discomfort.
    • Chemical Safety: Use of chemical-resistant PPE for volatile substances.
    • Physical Hazards: Lockout/tagout during equipment repair.
