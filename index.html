Two-Tiered Infection Control System

Standard Precautions: These are the minimum infection control practices used for all patients, regardless of their health status. They treat all blood and saliva as potentially infectious.

Transmission-Based Precautions: These are additional precautions for patients known or suspected to have specific infections spread through:

  • Airborne Transmission: (e.g., TB, measles).
  • Droplet Transmission: (e.g., mumps, influenza).
  • Contact Transmission: (e.g., herpes, Hepatitis A).
