A Two-Tiered Approach to Comprehensive Risk Control

Tier 1 Foundational/Universal Risk Controls

Tier 1: Foundational/Universal Risk Controls

These are the minimum, baseline safety practices used for all routine operations and individuals, regardless of specific known risks. They're the everyday precautions dental practices put in place:

  • Infection Control: This includes Standard Precautions like consistent hand hygiene, universal Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) such as gloves and masks, and thorough instrument sterilisation for all patients. It operates on the principle of treating all bodily fluids as potentially infectious.
  • Other Risks:
    • Ergonomics: We ensure universally ergonomic operatory setups and promote regular staff stretching to prevent strain.
    • Chemical Safety: This involves the secure storage and clear labelling of all chemicals, alongside general ventilation throughout the practice.
    • Physical Hazards: Keeping walkways clear, immediately cleaning up spills, and conducting routine equipment checks are essential to prevent accidents.

Tier 2 Enhanced/Specific Risk Controls

Tier 2: Enhanced/Specific Risk Controls

These are additional, targeted measures implemented when specific, elevated hazards are identified or suspected. They build upon the foundational controls, providing an extra layer of protection when needed:

  • Infection Control: This involves Transmission-Based Precautions, such as using P2 respirators for airborne pathogens or implementing isolation protocols when a patient is known or suspected to have a specific transmissible infection (e.g., airborne, droplet, or contact).
  • Other Risks:
    • Ergonomics: We use specialised ergonomic tools or workstation redesign when staff experience specific musculoskeletal discomfort, tailoring solutions to their needs.
    • Chemical Safety: This includes requiring specialised PPE, like chemical-resistant gloves or respirators, when handling highly volatile substances.
    • Physical Hazards: We implement specific lockout/tagout procedures for equipment undergoing repair to prevent accidental activation.
